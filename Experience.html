Experience

The journey began when I was offered the opportunity to continue my studies at the UiTM Rembau campus with my first choice course, Information Management. To summarise, the experience of starting the journey of university study was one I will never forget. I made a lot of new friends there, and I got to know my housemates. When we have free time, I spend a lot of time with my housemates. Some memories are on Friday night, we are having a sleepover and watched a movie together. On weekends, we also rarely go home, preferring to hang out outside the campus. In addition, we walked outside the campus area at night to eat. Also, we have been to Melaka at night to have our dinner together. We occasionally have a barbecue at the home of our housemate's uncle, who lives near the UiTM campus. In the afternoon, we'll unwind by jogging and enjoying the evening breeze in the campus area. Apart from making memories with my housemates, I also spend time with my close classmates. We will sit close together in class, and we will eat together after class in the cafe and dining area. We would sometimes hang out in the cafe late at night, chatting until late. It is a wonderful memory and experience to have new friends and acquaintances.

The incident occurred while I was attending an SPM seminar at Universiti Putra Malaysia (UPM). During the seminar camp's second night. I have an Islamic seminar class that I attend. After the isyak prayer time, the lesson begins at 9 a.m. In the middle of the lecture, I noticed a group of my close friends from another class panicked and reading a surah from their seats. Something weird seemed to be happening on there at the time. Then, after some time had passed, one of the friends was found lying down. Something had already possessed her. Her attitude had changed, and she began laughing continuously with laughter. It was terrifying to witness the situation. She got extremely unconscious as well as acted weirdly and creepily as the rest of the students in the halls panicked. The facilitator directed us to exit the hall and walk to the hall below. We were also asked to keep moving and avoid looking at the possessed girl. We had no choice but to leave our items on the table and make our way downstairs. I left my cell phone behind at the moment because I was so terrified of the possession changing other people's bodies. This is because I was having menstruation at the time, and I was really afraid of getting discouraged by the situation. We gathered with another seminar subject class below, and they were somewhat odd when it came to facing our fears. The teacher tried to shift our attention so that we wouldn't have to worry about what was going on. We began to sit in groups and recite surahs for as long as we needed to. However, my female friends began to cry because they were terrified and dismayed. Some even advised a friend to close the curtains in the window because he noticed someone staring at her from behind the curtains. Instead of staying in our individual dorms, we were told to sleep in the hall. I couldn't sleep at the moment because I was terrified of something happening down the hall. We had to go to the bathroom in groups as well. There were other friends in the hall who were rebellious and wanted to leave since they had heard the surah we recited. It had been the most horrific seminar experience I'd ever had. Until now, when there is a possession incident in front of me or all around me. I'll be terrified because I remember what happened right in front of my eyes. I'd start to feel discouraged and afraid. At the end, I'm hoping I don't have to go through this again.
